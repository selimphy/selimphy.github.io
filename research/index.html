Research

My research focuses on computational materials physics, where I use first-principles methods to understand how structural changes (especially phase transitions) modify the electronic structure and optical properties of functional oxide materials. A major theme in my work is connecting atomic-scale structure (crystal symmetry, bonding, distortions) to measurable properties such as band gaps, density of states, and optical spectra.

Methods: DFT • GW/QSGW • phonons • structure–property relationships • HPC workflows

Research themes

1) Phase transitions in oxide materials

  • Identify structural changes (symmetry, distortions, bonding) across phases.
  • Relate atomic rearrangements to electronic/optical signatures.
  • Use phonons and total energies to discuss stability and transition mechanisms.

2) DFT → GW/QSGW for electronic accuracy

  • Compare DFT and QSGW band structures and band gaps.
  • Analyze orbital character (p–d coupling, hybridization, band ordering).
  • Use QSGW when DFT underestimates key quasiparticle features.

3) Optical properties & spectra

  • Compute optical response and connect it to band transitions and symmetry.
  • Interpret spectral changes across phase/pressure/structure variations.

4) Tools & ecosystem

  • VASP, Ecalj (QSGW), Quantum ESPRESSO, Phonopy
  • Python (NumPy/Pandas/Matplotlib), gnuplot
  • VESTA/Jmol for structure visualization
