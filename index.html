About Me

I am a researcher in computational materials physics with academic training in physics and computational science. My work focuses on using first-principles methods to study how crystal structure, symmetry, and phase transitions influence the electronic and optical properties of functional materials.

I am currently pursuing my M.Sc. in Computational Science at Kanazawa University, Japan, where I work in the Theoretical and Computational Nanoscience Lab. My present research centers on zinc pyrovanadates and related oxide materials, with particular interest in DFT, QSGW, phonons, and structure–property relationships.

Research focus

  • Phase transitions in oxide materials
  • Electronic structure from DFT and QSGW
  • Optical properties and spectral interpretation
  • Phonon calculations and structural stability

Tools & methods

  • VASP, Ecalj, Quantum ESPRESSO, Phonopy
  • Python: NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib
  • Linux and HPC workflow development
  • Visualization with VESTA, Jmol, and gnuplot
