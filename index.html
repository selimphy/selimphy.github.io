About Me

I am an M.Sc. student in Computational Science at Kanazawa University (Japan), working in the Theoretical and Computational Nanoscience Lab. My research focuses on first-principles studies of phase transitions and the electronic/optical properties of oxide materials, with particular interest in zinc pyrovanadates. I use DFT and QSGW (Ecalj), along with tools such as Quantum ESPRESSO and Phonopy, and I build Linux/HPC workflows for simulation and analysis.

Tools: VASP • Ecalj • Quantum ESPRESSO • Phonopy • Python (NumPy/Pandas/Matplotlib) • Linux/HPC
