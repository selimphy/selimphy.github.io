Understanding QSGW intuitively

Physics Notes • March 2026

Quasiparticle self-consistent GW (QSGW) is an advanced electronic structure method used to improve the description of quasiparticle energies beyond density functional theory (DFT).

In standard GW calculations, the self-energy is constructed from the Green's function and screened Coulomb interaction. However, the starting point usually depends on a DFT calculation, which may introduce inaccuracies in the quasiparticle energies.

QSGW improves this by iteratively constructing a new effective potential so that the quasiparticle energies become self-consistent. In simple terms, the method replaces the exchange-correlation potential of DFT with a more physically accurate self-energy derived from many-body perturbation theory.

Because of this, QSGW often provides improved band gaps and electronic structures for many materials, especially correlated systems and semiconductors.
